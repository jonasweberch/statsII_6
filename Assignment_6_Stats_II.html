2. Key assumption of difference-in-differences estimation

The key assumption of the difference-in-differences estimation is that treatment and control groups would have evolved in parallel in the absence of treatment.

3. Visual evidence of key assumption

First, we created a sketch of the difference-in-differences (DiD) framework discussed in the lecture in order to illustrate the intuition behind the parallel trends assumption and the treatment effect.

Next, we assess whether the key assumption underlying the DiD approach, the parallel trends assumption, appears to hold in our data. In particular, we examine whether the two countries followed similar trends before the reform was implemented in 1993. The graph below suggests that this assumption is reasonably satisfied.

4. Formal evidence with regression

To formally test the parallel trends assumption, we estimate the following regression model using only pre-reform observations:

\[ CAREWORKERS_{it} = \alpha + \beta_1 YEAR_t + \beta_2 TREATED_i + \beta_3 (YEAR_t \times TREATED_i) + \varepsilon_{it} \]

#Setup DiD regression

pre_df <- subset(df, YEAR < 1993)

parallel_model <- lm(CAREWORKERS ~ YEAR * TREATED,
                     data = pre_df)

summary(parallel_model)

2. Main coefficient of interest

The main coefficient of interest is \(\beta_3\) (the coefficient on the interaction term \(POST \times TREATED\)), because it captures the difference-in-differences estimator which is the causal effect of the reform on the treated country (Country A) over and above any time trend or baseline group difference.

3. Interpretation of \(\hat\beta_3\)

\(\hat\beta_3\) measures the average change in the number of careworkers per 100 inhabitants in Country A after the reform, relative to the counterfactual trend captured by Country B which is the causal treatment effect of the tax exemption reform.

4. Interpretation of the other coefficients

Coefficient Interpretation
\(\hat{\beta}_0\) The average number of careworkers per 100 inhabitants in Country B before the reform.
\(\hat{\beta}_1\) (POST) The average change in careworkers after 1992 for Country B (the control group), capturing the common time trend.
\(\hat{\beta}_2\) (TREATED) The average difference in careworkers between Country A and Country B before the reform, capturing pre-existing baseline differences.

5. Was the reform succesful?

Yes, the reform was successful: the coefficient on the interaction term \(\hat{\beta}_3 = 0.728\) (POST:TREATED) is positive and highly statistically significant (\(t = 9.885\), \(p < 2 \times 10^{-16}\)), indicating that the tax exemption reform increased the number of careworkers per 100 inhabitants in Country A by approximately 0.73 additional careworkers relative to Country B after 1992.
